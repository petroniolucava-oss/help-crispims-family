My Story

I did not stop.
But the doors kept closing.

My name is Crispim. I married my wife Helena Isabel on December 9, 2020 — just after the pandemic ended. We built a small life together in Lubango, Angola. We had hope.

In August 2023, the company I worked for — Horizonte21 — went bankrupt and closed. I remember crying. I had a wife, a child, and no income. I did not know how we would eat.

During those dark months, I had a friend — a Church Elder and preacher. We would go together to a quiet place in the forest outside the city to pray. We shared our pain. We cried together with our arms around each other. That forest was my altar. I needed to keep my faith alive.

Someone gave me a chance to tutor their children at home. From December 2023 to October 2025, I taught. The money was very little — sometimes we still went hungry — but it was something.

In parallel, I decided I could not stay still. I began studying English alone, with no resources — using AI, Anki flashcards, and BBC Learning English videos. I studied 8 hours a day for 7 months because, as I say: the need spoke too loud for me to rest.

Then I borrowed the equivalent of $50 USD, paid for a development tool, bought a domain, and built a complete SaaS platform from scratch — an AI-powered WhatsApp appointment system for small businesses, with dashboards, CRM, and full automation. I built it with my own hands and mind.

But when I tried to reach clients, WhatsApp blocked my numbers for sending too many messages to strangers. Without money for paid advertising, I was stuck again. The product works. The door closed again.

I am not asking for a handout. I am asking for a bridge. A used car to work as a taxi driver — so I can feed my family while I keep building. With a car, I can also invest in advertising for the product I already created.

I have two children. My son turns 5 in September. My daughter just turned 1. I have looked at them and cried — not from despair, but from love, and from the weight of not being able to give them what they need. That love is why I am setting aside my pride today and asking.

The Why

She does not know what hunger is yet.

This is my daughter. She just turned one year old. She smiles like that every single day — not knowing what it costs to keep that smile alive.

My son is almost 5. He is curious, full of energy, and asks questions I love answering.

Every day I pray — not just for help, but that whoever finds this page will feel something real. I set aside time specifically for this prayer. I believe God moves hearts. Maybe He moved yours here today.
